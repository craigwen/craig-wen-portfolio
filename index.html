I'm a content designer specializing in high-stakes UX — account access, enforcement, and appeals — where the writing directly affects whether users can regain access to their account.

At Meta, I own end-to-end content strategy for flows handling over 100 million appeals per month across Facebook and Instagram, including identity verification, notifications, emails, and consent screens. Redesigning those screens to speak to users' specific situations, rather than generic policy messages, led to recovering 4.4 million legitimate users per year and reducing incorrectly impacted users by 50% in the US and Canada.

My work now includes designing AI systems. I've designed and launched a conversational AI assistant, and built automated quality judges to evaluate AI-generated content.

Outside of product work, I lead AI tools adoption across a 35-person content design org, driving AI-assisted coding tool adoption from 17% to 96% in one quarter. I also create high-fidelity, functional prototypes that replace static mocks for stakeholder reviews, and ship production code changes using AI-assisted development.
